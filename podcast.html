This module teaches trainees how to edit podcasts using Adobe Premiere Pro. It is designed for absolute beginners with no prior podcast or editing experience

The module covers both:

  • Long‚Äëform podcasts (full episodes)
  • Short‚Äëform podcast clips (for social media reels and shorts)

    LEARNING OBJECTIVES

    By the end of this module, trainees will be able to:

  • Understand what podcast editing is
  • Identify long‚Äëform vs short‚Äëform podcasts

    • PART 1: WHAT IS A PODCAST?

    A podcast is a recorded conversation, interview, or discussion that is shared as audio or video content.

    Podcasts can be:

  • Audio‚Äëonly
  • Video podcasts (with cameras)
  • Published as full episodes or short clips

    • PART 2: TYPES OF PODCAST CONTENT

    1: Long‚ÄëForm Podcast

  • Full episode
  • Usually 20 minutes to 2+ hours
  • Focused on conversation and clarity

    • 2. Short‚ÄëForm Podcast Clips

  • Short highlights taken from the long episode
  • Usually 15‚Äì90 seconds
  • Used for social media promotion
    • Important:
    Long‚Äëform focuses on clarity, short‚Äëform focuses on attention.

    PART 3: PREMIERE PRO INTERFACE (BEGINNER FRIENDLY)

    Common Podcast Files

  • Camera video files
  • Audio recorder files (WAV / MP3)

    • Intro / outro music

  • Beginner Rule
  • Always use external audio if available (microphones sound better than camera audio

    • PART 4: SETTING UP A PODCAST PROJECT IN PREMIERE PRO

    Step 1: Create a New Project

  • Name the project clearly
  • Save it in the correct project folder

    • Step 2: Import Media

  • Import all video files
  • Import all audio files
  • Import music files

    • PART 5: ORGANIZING PODCAST FILES (VERY IMPORTANT)

    Recommended Bin Structure

  • AUDIO ‚Äì microphones, recorders
  • MUSIC ‚Äì intro and outro
  • MUSIC ‚Äì intro and outro
  • SEQUENCES ‚Äì long‚Äëform and clips

    • Clean organization prevents confusion and mistakes.

    PART 6:SYNCHRONIZING AUDIO AND VIDEO (BEGINNER METHOD)

    When Sync Is Needed

  • Camera video + separate audio recorder

    • Simple Sync Method

  • Place video on the timeline
  • Place external audio below it
  • Line up audio waveforms
  • Mute or remove camera audio

    • PART 7: EDITING LONG‚ÄëFORM PODCASTS (STEP‚ÄëBY‚ÄëSTEP)

    Goal of Long‚ÄëForm Editing

  • Clear voices
  • Smooth conversation
  • Minimal distractions

    • Step 1: Listen First

  • Play the timeline
  • Understand the flow of conversation

    • Step 2: Clean the Audio

  • Remove long silences
  • Remove obvious mistakes (mic bumps, loud noise)

    • Step 3: Simple Cuts Only

  • Cut out mistakes or repeated words
  • Do NOT over‚Äëedit

    • Step 4: Add Intro & Outro

  • Place intro music at the beginning
  • Place outro music at the end

    • PART 8: BASIC AUDIO & MUSIC (INTRO LEVEL)

    Adding Music

  • Import music into the Project Panel
  • Drag music to an audio track (A1 or A2)

    • Audio Safety Rules

  • Avoid red audio meters (this means audio is too loud)
  • Dialogue should be clear and not overpowering

    • PART 9: BEGINNER EDITING PRACTICE

    Practice Task 1

  • Import provided training footage
  • Create a 30‚Äì45 second simple wedding montage
  • Use only:
    • Cuts
    • Music
    • Clean trimming

    Practice Task 2

  • Practice keyboard shortcuts:
    • Play / Pause
    • Cut clips
    • Zoom timeline

    PART 10: COMMON BEGINNER MISTAKES TO AVOID

  • Forgetting to save work
  • Leaving blank gaps in the timeline
  • Using too many effects
  • Editing without listening to audio
  • Renaming files outside Premiere Pro
